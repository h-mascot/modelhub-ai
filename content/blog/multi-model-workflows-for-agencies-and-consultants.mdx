# Multi-Model Workflows for Agencies and Consultants

Agency work is chaotic by design.
Every client wants something different, which means your AI workflow cannot be optimized for one narrow task.

## Why agencies outgrow single-model setups fast

One client needs website copy. Another needs a competitor analysis. Another needs meeting synthesis. Another needs a support workflow teardown.

No single model is the clean winner across all of that.

## Recommended workflow

### Research and source digestion
Use a model strong on long-context synthesis.

### Frameworks and structured deliverables
Use a model strong on organization and repeatable templates.

### Client-facing writing
Use a model strong on tone and readability.

## The economic angle

Agencies often make the mistake of letting every operator keep their own favorite subscription stack.
That feels flexible and becomes expensive quickly.

A shared multi-model workspace creates leverage because capability becomes team infrastructure instead of personal preference.

## Final takeaway

Agencies and consultants should treat AI like a studio workflow, not a random set of individual tabs.
That is exactly where aggregation becomes valuable.
