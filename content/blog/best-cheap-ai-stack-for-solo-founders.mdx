# Best Cheap AI Stack for Solo Founders

Solo founders need leverage, not vanity tooling.

The right AI stack helps you move faster across product, growth, support, and operations without turning into another monthly expense swamp.

## The real constraint is not intelligence

It is attention.
A solo founder needs tools that reduce switching, not just improve output quality in isolated cases.

## What the stack needs to cover

### Writing
Emails, landing pages, investor updates, docs.

### Strategy
Planning, prioritization, research synthesis.

### Execution
PRD drafts, support macros, launch checklists, bug triage.

### Coding help
Even non-technical founders increasingly need some implementation assistance.

## The most expensive stack is the fragmented one

Three separate subscriptions can look affordable individually and still create a clumsy daily workflow.
For many solo founders, an aggregator plus one or two specialized tools is more efficient than stacking broad AI plans endlessly.

## Recommended low-cost setup

### 1. One multi-model workspace
Use a product like ModelHub AI so you can switch across major model strengths without paying for several full-price direct plans.

### 2. One execution system
Use your existing docs, task manager, or code environment consistently. AI should fit into your workflow, not become the workflow.

### 3. Optional specialized tool only when it earns its place
Add a niche tool only if it saves time weekly, not just because it looked impressive in a thread.

## How to keep the stack cheap

- audit usage monthly
- kill duplicate plans quickly
- route by task, not hype
- avoid paying for backup subscriptions you rarely touch

## Final takeaway

The best cheap AI stack for solo founders is small, flexible, and boring in the best way. One clean multi-model system usually beats a pile of overlapping subscriptions.
